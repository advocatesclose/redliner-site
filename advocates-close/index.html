The Data Exists

Public Record

The information is not missing.
It is unprocessed.

The data to answer this question already exists. It has existed, in many cases, for over a century. The problem has never been access. The problem is processing.

The 1880 Census of the United States runs to 22 volumes. It enumerated every person, every farm, every factory, every dollar of assessed value in every county in every state. The Tenth Census was, in effect, a balance sheet of the nation.

The Federal Register published 106,109 pages in 2024. Every proposed rule, every final rule, every executive order, every notice — the complete output of the regulatory state, published daily.

Congress.gov indexes over 19,000 bills per Congress. Every amendment, every cosponsor, every floor statement transcribed and published in the Congressional Record. Two centuries of legislative history, structured and searchable.

EDGAR hosts millions of SEC filings. Every 10-K, every proxy statement, every registration statement filed by every public company in the United States. All freely available. All necessarily public.

The bottleneck is not information. It is the capacity to read it, parse it, reconcile it across formats and centuries and institutions — and to do so at a scale that makes the answers useful.

We build tools that process public data at scale. On your machine.

That is what Advocates Close does.

Architecture Decision

Everything runs on your machine.

No server. No API key. No data leaves your hardware. This is not a legal position — it is an engineering choice. On-device processing is the right architecture for sensitive data. The compute is local. The storage is local. The models are local. We build it this way because it is the better way to build it.
